What's the Story

here is a little blurb on how and why this page exists.

When did this all start?

Well, I'm a software developer (also for web sites) and am interested in the latest standards and cool web technologies that continiously grow.  I was looking into AJAX and stumbled upon Babble.  I played it a few times, love it, invited my wife to try it out.

Why did you decide to create clues?

One Sunday my mrpresident was struggling with Babble and was patiently waiting for some two letter clues.  I looked at the grid and a big challenge was tempting me. Can I do it? Can I write a piece of software to create clues?

How long did it take?

I would say I spent 4 hours on the first day, and then 3 more hours over the next month or so.  There where a few hurdles to get across - like Qu words and also to speed it up - 4 hours was just too long!  On average it's about 10mins to get the clues done.

How does it work?

I started with a plan. Lets get all possible words into a big list, and go through all possible combinations of connecting letters in a Babble grid and work out which ones are there, and where they sit.

I sucked all the words sized 4 letters to 11 letters from the internet, put it all into a Database.

There are some cool logic in solving, doing things like :

1) Can't turn back on yourself - reusing the same letter twice.

2) Have to test each letter for each word length - as some words don't have smaller variations.

3) Doing some pretesting for any words starting with certain combinations to speed it up.

What do you do each day?

When a Babble grid flips, I type all the grid letters into my program. I then hit Solve, and it finds all the words and then spits them out as clues.

I then paste this into the web page.

I also click another button to create the boxless clues, and page that into another web. 

It's not completely automated yet - haven't found a way to grab the letters from the grid automatically. Not sure if I would do it if I solved that one.

Do you cheat?

No, it is not in my character to cheat. In fact, I rarely have the time to play babble - Many many things on all at once!  I also don't help anyone else to cheat - what's the point!

Do you know dr_god or have an association with www.playbabble.com? 

No.  The only contact I've had with dr_god is to congratulate him on his creation, and request a link be added for the clues to be on 'friends of babble'.

I've upgraded to Pro Babbler and promote others to contribute to the game to keep it coming.

The only profits I get is the lovely comments from all the fellow babblers.
